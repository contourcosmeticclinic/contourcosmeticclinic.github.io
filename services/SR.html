Understanding Scars and Why They Form

A scar is the body's natural way of healing after skin damage. When the deep layer of the skin (dermis) is injured, the body produces collagen fibers to repair the wound, resulting in a scar. The appearance of a scar depends on several factors:

  • Type of Injury: Cuts, burns, surgical incisions, acne, or chickenpox.
  • Location on the Body: Scars on joints or areas of tension may stretch or become more prominent.
  • Individual Healing Tendency: Genetics play a significant role.
  • Age: Younger skin tends to scar more aggressively.
  • Skin Type/Pigmentation: Darker skin tones are more prone to keloid or hypertrophic scarring.

Types of Scars We Treat at Contour Cosmetic Clinic

Our expertise at Contour Cosmetic Clinic in Bangalore encompasses a wide range of scar types, including:

  • Discoloration or Surface Irregularities: Scars that are flat but differ in color or texture from the surrounding skin.
  • Hypertrophic Scars: Raised, red, and often itchy scars that remain within the boundaries of the original wound. They may improve over time but often require treatment for faster resolution.
  • Keloid Scars: More aggressive than hypertrophic scars, keloids are raised, firm, often itchy, and extend beyond the original wound boundaries. They can continue to grow larger over time.
  • Contracture Scars: Often result from burns or injuries that cause significant tissue loss. These scars pull the edges of the skin together, restricting movement, especially when they cross a joint.
  • Acne Scars: Various types, including ice pick, boxcar, and rolling scars, which create depressions or irregularities on the skin's surface.
  • Surgical Scars: Scars from operations, which may be wide, raised, or poorly healed.
  • Traumatic Scars: Scars resulting from accidents or injuries.

Advanced Scar Revision Techniques Offered in Bangalore

At Contour Cosmetic Clinic, we utilize a combination of cutting-edge techniques and established methods to achieve optimal scar revision results:

1. Topical Treatments & Injectables:

  • Silicone Gels/Sheets: Often the first line of defense for new scars to flatten and soften them.
  • Corticosteroid Injections: Effective for reducing inflammation, size, and itching of hypertrophic and keloid scars.
  • 5-Fluorouracil (5-FU) Injections: Used for stubborn keloids, often in combination with steroids.

2. Laser Therapy:

  • Pulsed Dye Lasers (PDL): Excellent for reducing redness and improving the texture of new and hypertrophic scars.
  • Fractional Lasers (Ablative and Non-Ablative): Target the scar tissue, stimulating collagen remodeling and improving texture, color, and depth for various scar types, including acne scars and surgical scars.
  • Q-Switched Lasers: Can be used for pigment correction in discolored scars.

3. Surgical Scar Revision:

  • Excision: Removing the scar and carefully re-closing the wound to create a less noticeable scar. This is often done for wide, depressed, or raised scars.
  • Z-Plasty/W-Plasty: Surgical techniques that involve reshaping the scar to redirect tension, break up a straight line, and make it less conspicuous, especially effective for contracture scars.
  • Skin Grafts/Flaps: In severe cases, especially after extensive burns or trauma, healthy skin may be transplanted from another part of the body to cover the scarred area.

4. Dermabrasion / Microneedling:

  • Dermabrasion: A controlled surgical sanding of the top layers of the skin to smooth out raised or irregular scars.
  • Microneedling (Collagen Induction Therapy): Uses fine needles to create micro-injuries, stimulating collagen production and improving the texture and appearance of atrophic (depressed) scars, like some acne scars.

5. Adjunctive Therapies:

  • Pressure Garments: Used for burn scars or large hypertrophic/keloid scars to apply constant pressure and prevent recurrence.
  • Cryotherapy: Freezing the scar tissue, particularly effective for small keloids.

Important Considerations

  • Realism: It's crucial to have realistic expectations. While scar revision can significantly improve the appearance and symptoms of a scar, it cannot completely remove it.
  • Time: Scars typically take 12-18 months to fully mature. Often, it's best to wait until a scar has matured before undergoing revision, though some early interventions can prevent severe scarring.
  • Multiple Sessions: Many scar revision treatments, especially non-surgical ones, require a series of sessions for best results.
