What Exactly is Xeomin and How Does It Work?

Xeomin, short for Botulinum Toxin Type A, is a purified protein derived from the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. When injected in tiny, controlled amounts into specific facial muscles, it temporarily blocks nerve signals that cause those muscles to contract.

It's these repeated muscle contractions – from frowning, squinting, or raising eyebrows – that eventually lead to "dynamic wrinkles" or "expression lines" etched into the skin. By relaxing these muscles, Xeomin allows the overlying skin to smooth out, reducing the appearance of existing wrinkles and preventing new ones from forming.

Areas Commonly Treated with Xeomin at Contour Cosmetic Clinic

Xeomin is incredibly versatile and can effectively treat a range of areas to create a more relaxed and rejuvenated appearance:

  • Forehead Lines: Horizontal lines that appear when you raise your eyebrows.
  • Frown Lines (Glabellar Lines): The vertical lines between your eyebrows that create a "scowling" or "angry" look.
  • Crow's Feet: The fine lines radiating from the corners of your eyes, often visible when you smile or squint.
  • Bunny Lines: Wrinkles that appear on the bridge of the nose when you scrunch it.
  • Brow Lift: A subtle lift to the eyebrows can be achieved by relaxing specific muscles that pull the brows down.
  • Lip Lines (Smoker's Lines): Fine vertical lines around the mouth.
  • Gummy Smile: By relaxing the upper lip muscle, Xeomin can reduce the amount of gum visible when smiling.
  • Chin Dimpling (Pebble Chin): Smoothing out the 'orange peel' appearance of the chin.
  • Jawline Slimming (Masseter Reduction): For those with a wide jawline due to overdeveloped masseter muscles, Xeomin can lead to a more oval or V-shaped facial contour.
  • Neck Bands (Platysmal Bands): Vertical cords that become prominent on the neck with age.
  • Excessive Sweating (Hyperhidrosis): Xeomin can significantly reduce sweating in areas like the underarms, palms, and soles.
  • Migraine Prevention: For chronic migraine sufferers, Xeomin injections can help reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.

Important Considerations

  • Safety: When administered by a qualified and experienced practitioner, Xeomin is a very safe procedure. Rare side effects can include temporary bruising, swelling, headache, or, very rarely, temporary drooping of an eyelid or eyebrow.
  • Not for Static Wrinkles: Xeomin primarily addresses dynamic wrinkles caused by muscle movement. For static wrinkles (those present even at rest), dermal fillers or other skin rejuvenation treatments might be more suitable, often used in combination with Xeomin for comprehensive results.
  • Not for Pregnant/Breastfeeding Women: Xeomin is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women.
