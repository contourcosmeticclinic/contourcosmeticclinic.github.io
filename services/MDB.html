What is Microdermabrasion and How Does It Work?

Microdermabrasion is a gentle mechanical exfoliation technique that utilizes a specialized device to buff away the superficial layer of dead and damaged skin cells (the stratum corneum). There are primarily two methods:

  1. Crystal Microdermabrasion: Fine crystals (like aluminum oxide or sodium bicarbonate) are sprayed onto the skin through a handpiece, simultaneously vacuuming away the crystals and the exfoliated skin cells.
  2. Diamond-Tip Microdermabrasion: A diamond-tipped wand is moved across the skin, gently abrading the surface, while a vacuum suction removes the dead skin cells. This method is often preferred for sensitive areas or around the eyes.

Both methods work on the principle of controlled exfoliation. By removing the dead skin barrier, microdermabrasion not only instantly reveals brighter skin but also stimulates blood flow and encourages the production of new skin cells, leading to a healthier, more vibrant complexion over time.

Is Microdermabrasion Right for You?

Microdermabrasion is suitable for most skin types, including sensitive skin, as its intensity can be adjusted. It's an excellent choice if you're looking for:

  • Improvement in skin texture, tone, and radiance.
  • Reduction in the appearance of fine lines and superficial scars.
  • Clearer pores and fewer breakouts.
  • Enhanced absorption of your favorite skincare products.
