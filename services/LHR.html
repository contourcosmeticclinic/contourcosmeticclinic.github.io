The Science of Smooth: How Laser Hair Removal Works

Laser Hair Removal is a medical procedure that uses a concentrated beam of light (laser) to target and damage hair follicles. Here's the science behind it:

  • Targeting Melanin: The laser emits a specific wavelength of light that is absorbed by the pigment called melanin, which is found in the hair shaft. Darker hair has more melanin, making it an ideal target.
  • Heat Generation: When the melanin absorbs the laser light, it converts into heat. This heat travels down the hair shaft to the hair follicle, where it damages the follicle's ability to grow new hair.
  • Hair Growth Cycle: Hair grows in cycles (Anagen - active growth, Catagen - transitional, Telogen - resting). Laser hair removal is most effective on hairs that are in the active growth (Anagen) phase, as these follicles contain the most melanin. Since not all hairs are in the Anagen phase at the same time, multiple sessions are required to target all hair follicles as they cycle into the active growth stage.

The result is a significant and long-lasting reduction in unwanted hair, with any regrowth being finer, lighter, and sparser.

  6. Results & Maintenance: Over the weeks following each session, treated hairs will shed. You'll notice a gradual reduction in hair growth. Any remaining hair will be finer and lighter. Maintenance sessions, typically once or twice a year, may be recommended to keep the area completely smooth.

Is Laser Hair Removal Right for You?

Laser Hair Removal is generally effective for individuals with dark hair and light skin, but with advanced laser technologies like the Nd:YAG, it is now safely and effectively performed on darker skin tones as well, which is a significant advantage for the Indian population. It is not effective on very light-colored hair (blonde, red, grey, white) as these hairs lack sufficient melanin to absorb the laser light.
